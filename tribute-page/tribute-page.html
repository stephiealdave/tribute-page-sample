George Gordon Byron (Lord Byron)

British Nobleman, Poet, and Politician

Lord Byron painted by Thomas Phillips in 1813. Venizelos Mansion, Athens (the British Ambassador's residence)

George Gordon Byron is my most favorite British poet I had ever known. I spent my childhood to early teenage years reading thick literature books that is why I knew him. Long time ago, I chose him to be my main topic for a school project. I found it enjoyable, to type his poems stanza by stanza though it's long. Among his poems, She Walks in Beauty is what I like the most.

She walks in beauty, like the night
Of cloudless climes and starry skies;
And all that’s best of dark and bright
Meet in her aspect and her eyes;
Thus mellowed to that tender light
Which heaven to gaudy day denies.

One shade the more, one ray the less,
Had half impaired the nameless grace
Which waves in every raven tress,
Or softly lightens o’er her face;
Where thoughts serenely sweet express,
How pure, how dear their dwelling-place.

And on that cheek, and o’er that brow,
So soft, so calm, yet eloquent,
The smiles that win, the tints that glow,
But tell of days in goodness spent,
A mind at peace with all below,
A heart whose love is innocent!

Lord Byron's Timeline

  • 1788: Born, 16 Holles Street, London.
  • 1798: Became 6th Baron Byron of Rochdale on death of great-uncle.
  • 1805: Went up to Trinity College, Cambridge University.
  • 1806: Collection of poems, Fugitive Pieces, privately printed.
  • 1807: Second volume of poetry, Poems on Various Occasions, privately printed. Hours of Idleness published.
  • 1808: Poems Original and Translated published.
  • 1809: Took seat in the House of Lords. English Bards and Scotch Reviewers Published.
  • 1810: Toured Turkey, visited Smyrna, Ephesus, Troy.
  • 1811: Wrote Hints from Horace and The Curse of Minerva.
  • 1812: Childe Harold's Pilgrimage Cantos I and II published, which made him famous overnight.
  • 1813: First edition of The Giaour published.
  • 1814: Corsair published. 10,000 copies sold on day of publication. Ode to Napoleon Buonaparte published anonymously.
  • 1815: Married to Miss Annabella Milbanke at Seaham. Wrote Siege of Corinth and Parisina.
  • 1816: Childe Harold III and Prisoner of Chillon and Other Poems published.
  • 1817: Manfred, Lament published.
  • 1818: Childe Harold IV published.
  • 1819: Don Juan I and II published anonymously.
  • 1821-1822: He joined with Leigh Hunt and Percy Bysshe Shelley in starting a short-lived newspaper, The Liberal.
  • 1823: He accepted overtures for his support from representatives of the movement for Greek independence from the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1824: Died of Malaria.

If you want to know more details about his life and works, visit his Wikipedia page.
